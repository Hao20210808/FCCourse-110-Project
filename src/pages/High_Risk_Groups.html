Occupation with potential exposure

Occupational exposure to SARS-CoV-2 virus can occur in the workplace, during business trips to community transmission areas, and on the way to and from get off work, so workplace exposure risk depends on the type of industry, the need for close or repeated contact with confirmed or suspected cases, and the risk of contact with contaminated surfaces, etc.
With reference to the national definitions of high disease risk industries in the annex, and the Occupational Safety and Health Administration, Workplace exposure by OSHA and World Health Organization The classification of risks, this guideline lists the occupations with potential exposure as follows:


  1. Medical personnel with practicing registration.
  2. Medical institutions, hemodialysis units, nursing institutions (including home nursing homes, general nursing homes, spiritual nursing homes, and postpartum nursing homes), welfare institutions for the elderly (including long-term care institutions, nursing homes, etc.), residential physical and mental Non-medical personnel in disability welfare institutions, residential long-term care institutions, residential mental rehabilitation institutions, dementia group homes, honorary national homes, and other related workplaces.
  3. Personnel from centralized quarantine stations, epidemic prevention accommodation, screening stations, epidemic prevention fleets, and other related operations and epidemic prevention facilities who are in contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients.
  4. Meal delivery service workers who have been in contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients, including village chiefs or village officers.
  5. Frontline public health personnel involved in outbreak investigation and contact tracing.
  6. Perform specimen collection or operator on confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients.
  7. First responders, including firefighters, emergency medical technicians, paramedics, ambulance drivers, air ambulance crews.
  8. Police officers, soldiers, coast guards and coast guards, and correctional institution staff who perform epidemic prevention-related operations or come into contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients.
  9. Forensic, forensic and other on-site personnel who examine or dissect the remains or perform judicial examinations, and undertakers who bury, incinerate, or have direct contact with the remains.
  10. Other jobs or tasks with a high probability of close contact with confirmed or suspected cases of COVID-19, or contact with surfaces that may be contaminated with the virus.
  11. Persons with significant or high transmission due to work entry and exit locations that meet the community transmission indicators.
  • Personnel from centralized quarantine centers, epidemic prevention accommodation, screening stations, epidemic prevention fleets, and other related operations and epidemic prevention facilities who have not been in contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients.
  • Food delivery staff who have not been in contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients, including village chiefs or village officers.
  • Providers of home-based care services include domestic workers, family caregivers and home-care staff, as well as home-based care workers, temporary care workers, personal assistants, etc. who are actually engaged in home care.
  • Community-based care service providers, including staff of day service agencies, day care centers, community-based long-term care agencies, community-based services, Evergreen Academy, and other related workplaces.
  • Providers of transportation services for medical treatment, rehabilitation, and care services, such as rehabilitation bus drivers and on-board staff.
  • Police, military personnel, coast guards and coast guards, and correctional institution staff who have not had direct contact with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 patients.
  • Judicial service personnel on duty.
  • Front-line workers in customs, immigration, quarantine, security, etc. for border control staff.
  • Mass transit drivers and other workers.
  • Air crew, merchant marine crew.
  • Transportation and warehousing operators.
  • Retail store staff.
  • Hospitality workers.
  • Restaurant staff.
  • Workers in food processing, manufacturing, distribution, retail, farm work, meat packaging, etc.
  • School staff and on-campus staff.
  • Education and security service personnel of kindergarten education and security service institutions.
  • Professionals of child care centers, early care institutions, placement and correctional institutions, and other welfare institutions or groups for children and children.
  • Other jobs require frequent or close contact with the public, colleagues, visitors, customers, contractors, etc., but do not require contact with confirmed or suspected cases of COVID-19.
