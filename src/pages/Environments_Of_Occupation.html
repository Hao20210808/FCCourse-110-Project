Risk factors affecting transmission include:

  1. Exposure length and frequency (time);
  2. Proximity or physical contact with an infected person (nonlinear relationship with distance);
  3. The number of people in the same space (the probability of an infected person, and the possibility of a second infection);
  4. Individual infectivity (different virus strains are also different);
  5. Virus excretion rate (there are individual differences and differences in activities);
  6. The proportion of the virus transmitted by close-range droplets, long-distance aerosol particles, and object surfaces (there are individual differences and differences in the scene);
  7. The use and effect of control methods, including ventilation and exhaust, social distancing, hygiene measures, etc.;
  8. Socioeconomic factors (affecting the ability to self-isolate, such as housing size).
